# Original Description
Approximately on November 17-18, the Monero website offered compromised binaries to users. We needed to get the information out quickly to users who may have downloaded infected binaries.

We need a set of guides, processes, and contacts for important news to be shared in the case of emergencies. The rest of this issue is simply a data dump.

**Ways to Get the Word Out**

1. getmonero.org
2. r/Monero, r/MoneroMining, r/xmrtrader, and other related subreddits
3. #monero-community, #monero, #monero-dev, #monero-research-lab, and other IRC channels
4. Blockfolio Signal
5. CoinGecko Beam
6. Delta Direct
7. @monero Twitter
8. Monero Facebook
9. Monero-announce email
10. Monero Telegram

We should keep an updated list of who is subscribed to which platform type and how effective the news is distributed. We should also run tests to see visibility.

**Safe Disclosure Process**

The recent website issue was disclosed on GitHub. We should look at the VRP and other methods to see if there are better ways to get the information out and escalated as necessary.

**Dedicated Workgroup/Committee/Role**

Given the nature of the distributed community and that emergencies can happen at any time, we need a robust network of committed, reachable, knowledgeable volunteers who take ownership of this process. They should be easily contactable and be able to handle sensitive cases. For example, I was nearly asleep when I received a notification from binaryFate in this case, and if I was asleep, then contact methods 4-7 may not have gone out for another 7 hours.

**Regular Tests**

There should be an "internal audit"-like function that regularly suggests process improvements and tests the procedures for their effectiveness.

---

Please comment/react if you are interested in helping with a draft response process.
