Last update from OP on Bitcointalk.org (Aug 2025) suggests this was perhaps overzealous policy but the fact that they refunded him after pressure is enough of a red flag that they're being selective with policy application. You don't return funds that trigger a KYC/AML check until said checks are done, either the funds are suspicious - in which case you hold + ask for verification or they never were and ID never needed to be provided and your checks were wrong - but you never return funds that are frozen without verification of identity or identifying if it was a false positive (this covers the financial institute ass as well)
