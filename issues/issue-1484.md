## erciccione | 2021-03-01T17:52:01+00:00
> I believe that the frequency of re-design shouldn't be the sole factor that prevents a re-design from occurring

@tallest-man it's not. I'm ok with aimed redesigns if there are useful improvements suggested and it's not just a "i like this text better" kind of situation. Beside that, two important additional factors should be considered:

- The new strings will need to be translated again. The text of some pages were changed not long ago. Nobody suggested improvements at the time, so the changes went in as they were. Then, translators started to work on these new strings. Some of these translators were also funded through CCS proposals. Changing strings means translators' work will be wasted. Now think that (almost) every page on this website is available in 13 languages and how many people you need to translate everything (even if for most people translations simply happen, there is a huge amount of work and coordination in the background between me and the translators. Without even considering the work necessary on Weblate itself).
- The proposed changes will need to be implemented by somebody. I'm ok with applying the changes if people want them, but that would take out time from other important works that are still in progress (like converting the entire user guides and moneropedia to translate them on Weblate). Even if somebody else would take care of making the changes, reviewing them will still take considerable time.

To reiterate: I'm ok with making substantial changes, but only when really necessary. Also, the amount of resources to invest (for translators, reviewers and committers) should be considered.

In this case, personally i'm ok with adding the buttons or changing the header, even if i don't consider that a priority or something particularly useful, but i don't like the new content (as explained in my comment above) that is being proposed and i don't think it worth spending so much time and resources for it.
