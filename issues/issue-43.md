## rehrar | 2017-07-26T18:17:27+00:00
Alright, so I've got a number of ideas as to how something like this can be done. I'll list them below:

- Outreach to university CS programs. Whether it's giving a small presentation or even seeing if a professor wouldn't mind giving some credit for contributing to the project in some way. This would entail some sort of oversight for the student, so it may be worthwhile to think of a way that it can benefit the student without putting strain on the lead devs of the project.
- Encourage involvement in other areas of the Open Source world which can involve such things as:
  - Having a few volunteers in the Open Source Initiative (OSI) in https://opensource.org (perhaps a donation in Monero here as well?)
  - Building deliberate bridges of relationship between us and other honest, open source, privacy-centered programs including seeing about integrations, accepting Monero (offering free help), or making community-raised donations in Monero to said projects.
- Volunteering to write articles or other high quality material for various open source platforms and ideas (i.e. [opensource.com](https://opensource.com)).
- Identifying communities of open source developers and reaching out to them
