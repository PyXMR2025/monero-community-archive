> The confirmations were just climbing up and up and they still are as of today.
> ...when I look at the transaction in my wallet or on XMRchain, it is still being
confirmed.

This is expected behavior when a transaction is sucessfully sent. Since your transaction has 4557 confirmations, it means that 4556 blocks were mined after the block that included your transaction, so it was definitely sent (it takes ~2 minutes for each block to be mined).

>  I have others that went through successfully and they don't keep getting confirmed like these two.

This behavior is not expected. Once confirmed, a succesful transaction should add new confirmations forever.

> Furthermore, my wallet says "unknown recipient" instead giving the recipient's XMR address like it usually does once it is completed. 
>  One of my two transactions, the first one that I listed, it did look like it completed about 9 hours later than I needed it to.  It showed a recipient address.  But then, a few
days later, that address changed back to "unknown recipient" in my wallet.

If your wallet displays "unknown recipient" after some time, it means it lost this information, which is stored in its cache file. Monero GUI wallet always saves the destination address of each transaction in its cache. Usually what makes the wallet lose its cache file is restoring/refreshing the wallet from seed or changing wallet restore height.

Which Monero wallet did you use to send these transactions? Was it GUI wallet?
If so, did you refresh/recover your wallet from seed after sending these transactions?
If you're not using GUI wallet, and for instance, is using cake wallet in mobile, you need to activate in settings page the option to save destination addresses.

> The exchange has no reason to deny that the transfer came through. They validated the other ones. 

All cryptocurrency scammers work this way: first they do 1, 2 or 3 successful exchanges to gain customer confidence, then, when you decide to do a larger transaction, they get your money and disappear.

> My assumption is that they reject any transactions that take longer than three hours.  Is that something a recipient can do?  Can a transaction be rejected by the receiver? 

They can rejected the transaction in their system (and refuse to do the exchange) because the exchange rate is not updated after some time. But since it's not possible to reject a transaction in the monero protocol, it's not possible for a monero wallet to decline receiving funds. If you sent them a transaction and the transaction was confirmed, your funds are now in their wallet. Cryptocurrency transactions are atomic, that means that the funds are either in the wallet of the sender or in the wallet of the receiver, they don't stay in a temporary place between both wallets.

If the exchange you used is honest, they should refund you once you paid them and received nothing in return. You should contact them, but there is nothing we can do to help you in this sense (the fund is already in their wallet and transactions are irreversible). If you still have the destination address of the transaction (if you can't find it in your wallet anymore, maybe you can recover it from the exchange website), you will be able to prove to the exchange that you sent them these transactions. You can use this tool (https://www.exploremonero.com/receipt) to do this, just fill the fields with the transaction id, the destination address and the transaction key (you can find this in History page if you use Monero GUI wallet).

> You mentioned using an escrow account with a trusted third party.  I had
assumed that that's what I was doing because of how the system is set up. 

No, what you did was paying directly to someone else's wallet. 

> Is there an external escrow service I can utilizee instead of relying on the exchange to hold my funds until finalized?  

A multisignature transaction is a transaction that needs multiple signatures to be send (like 2-of-3 signatures). So there are 3 users (and 3 keys) involved in the transaction: the buyer, the seller and an arbiter. You can see this type of scheme when using exchanges like https://bisq.network/ to do exchanges.

I'd also recommend checking the [services that are distrusted by the community](https://www.reddit.com/r/monero/wiki/avoid/) and should be avoided.

## JustFranz | 2021-02-23T06:26:08+00:00
The XMR will arrive in the recipent wallet as soon as a miner has included your transaction in a block that they mined. Monero blocks are mined every 2 minutes and since Monero blocks are not full it should not take more than 2 minutes to get a confirmation.

If a transaction is sent but gets dropped then what likely happened is a node dropped your transaction in the Dandelion++ stem phase. Dandelion++ is a technology that helps obfuscate the origin IP of a Monero transaction. If that happens then send the transaction again. I am not aware of an active attack on the Monero network that is maliciously dropping transactions, it could happen at any time though.

A Monero transaction should not take more than 2 minutes. For you to see the transaction you need to verify the blockchain from the point you last stopped verifying. Depending on when that was it can take quite a while. In the case of a remote node you need to scan all the transactions for ones that are meant for you, that can take a while. The timestamp of the first confirmation is when the Monero arrived in the receiving wallet. If there is a delay between that and you seeing it in your wallet then the problem is your wallet. It is either not synced, is not syncing at all, you are not connected to a remote node in the first place or your wallet restore height is set to after the first confirmation of the transaction.

Or your seed is compromised and the money was already sent out, that can happen too. Never use web wallets, never generate a seed on a website and never give out your seed. Use a secure computer and operating system to safeguard your money.

I've experienced in the past where the Official GUI will stop syncing, freeze, not indicate what it is doing, won't tell you when a remote node is down and other annoyances.

Try out the Feather Wallet, it is light weight and easier to use. featherwallet dot org is the address. The development was funded by the Monero community and you can trust the software.


Those two transactions that you sent went through. Contact them about all the details for support. If they deny receiving them then you got scammed.

The Monero devs or devs of any decentralized cryptocurrency can not reverse or refund transactions. They just make the software. You own and are responsible for your coins.
