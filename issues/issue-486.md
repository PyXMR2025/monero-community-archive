## TheFuzzStone | 2020-07-17T16:42:14+00:00
I would like to ask the Monero community to discuss my future CCS-proposal.

I have already consulted with Justin (@SamsungGalaxyPlayer) and Diego (@rehrar) and shared my thoughts with them to hear their opinions.

Below I present to you my thoughts. Please remember that this is **not** the final version of my future CCS-proposal, these are my thoughts that I want to share with the Monero community to get feedback.

---

For those who do not know me, I am TheFuzzStone (contacts + PGP-key are [here](https://thefuzzstone.github.io/)), co-founder of the biggest local Monero-community: [XMR.RU](https://xmr.ru/)

Since 2017, our XMR.RU-team has been actively involved in the development and spreading information about Monero among the [CIS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States) countries.

Recently I decided to submit my CCS-proposal, to be able to allocate more time to Monero.

Basically, my CSS-proposal can be called the parallel to [this one](https://repo.getmonero.org/monero-project/ccs-proposals/-/merge_requests/154), because we will continue to work together. 

When he translates some stuff and makes a pull request on Github, he always pings me as a reviewer, and with the funding I will be able to spend more time on all reviews.

For example, I did the [Mastering Monero review](https://github.com/monerobook/monerobook/pull/81), and I was doing it for a very long time, because I have to do other stuff to earn money for myself. I will be more useful to the community if I am not busy with other things.

Since 2017, we at XMR.RU have developed a scheme that works very well. An article (or other type of content) is translated by one person, and another person review it with the "fresh eyes", because it's impossible not to make mistakes, no matter how cool the translator you are.

I also want to pay special attention to Ukrainian language in Monero. The fact is that since 2014 to this day in Ukraine is a complicated situation (political stuff), and a certain part of the population is against Russian language, so I consider the translation of GUI and CLI into Ukrainian mandatory to keep Monero out of politics in the eyes of certain people.
There is a GUI translation into Ukrainian, but as GUI is constantly evolving, this translation needs to be improved and kept up to date.

I also noticed that MyMonero is starting to gain popularity among lightweight wallets, so MyMonero will be translated into Ukrainian as well.

I also want to make the Monero community understand that my proposal is not a simple "funding of a translator", but a full-fledged contributor in every sense of the word. Because translating a certain material is not a problem, but supporting translations (especially wallets and manuals) takes time and effort.

Also my work will consist the development and maintenance of other important services in the Monero ecosystem, for example our lovely XMR.to - at the moment they do not have translation into Russian/Ukrainian, which creates certain problems for some Russian-speaking users. Some people don't understand this service at all, or why it is needed.

The Monero community (donors) will be able to track my activity on Github, and for more convenient and detailed monitoring of my activity (backed up by links) I'll create a public Trello (or its FOSS analogue).

Also, during my working hours (when I am online, which is almost every day I spend 8-10 hours in front of my laptop) I will be available to the English speaking part of the Monero community to coordinate any actions and/or urgent alerts to the Russian-speaking community.

Anybody can always text me:

My Telegram ID is **253373664**, please forward any of my messages to bot [@userinfobot](https://telegram.me/userinfobot) to verify it. There are scammers in Telegram who pretend to be me.)

- Matrix: [@TheFuzzStone:matrix.org](https://matrix.to/#/@TheFuzzStone:matrix.org)

I read and respond to absolutely all the messages (it's a habit).

---

**What else will I do for the Monero?**

- Creation of new manuals as well as keeping the old ones up to date.

I like to create user guides for beginners, and I think I'm good at it.
You all know that you can't take a screenshot in a Monerujo wallet... but I did it, and I think [my guide is good](https://xmr.ru/manuals/monerujo-wallet-android/).

I'll create the same detailed and friendly guides for beginners for Cake Wallet, MyMonero, EDGE wallet.

About other wallets. If the wallet is not open source, I personally do not use it, and I do not recommend it to others. The same principle is supported by the majority of our community. 

- Translation of [GetMonero.org](https://web.getmonero.org/) into Ukrainian and keeping it up to date (including Moneropedia, user guides, etc.)

- Distributing news and information about Monero on various resources.

I have a good reputation on two most important crypto-forums among Ukrainians/Russians/Belarusians (bitcointalk.org and forum.bits.media), so I always promptly post there important news about Monero, as well as translations from our XMR.RU-team, and provide support if people need it. 
Since 2017, I've invested some time in growing this page ("Puplication" as they call it) on Medium (https://medium.com/xmr-ru), and this has brought some success. The statistics for the last 90 days:

* The total amount of time spent reading XMR.RU publication: 1146 minutes
* The total number of views XMR.RU publication has received on all posts and pages: 846

Although Medium is centralized and proprietary, I use it to spread information about Monero among Russian-speaking users as Medium is very well indexed by Google and Yandex. Also I have some future plans to use other similar popular platforms to spread information about Monero.

I'm also a big fan of the [Fediverse](https://fediverse.party/) (it's free and open source space with a lot of interesting people). Its popularity is growing more and more, so, I have some plans for spreading information about Monero in the [Fediverse](https://fediverse.party/).
