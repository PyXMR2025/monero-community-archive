I think `static std::unique_ptr<device_registry> registry{new device_registry};` (inside of `get_device_registry`) is equivalent. AFAIK, compilers typically use `atexit` to destruct local static objects - the standard mandates that local static destructors and `atexit` functions be sequenced in reverse order.
