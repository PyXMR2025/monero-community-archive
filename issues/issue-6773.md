# Original Description
Somewhat related to #6772 (in that the benefits of having this functionality become apparent only with larger address books), and assuming the same basic scenario (imagine you have 40 entries in the address book), consider the following..

Often it is the case whilst looking up an addressbook entry that you either know *exactly* what you're looking for, or at least you remember the category (is it a friend? business acquaintance? family? your other wallet addresses?).

Based on that empirical observation, I think that monero GUIs and CLI should support **searching** for addressbook entries (arguably this feature does not belong in monero-wallet-cli though) and allowing the user to **categorize** entries.

This flows nicely with the idea proposed in #6772 also, since categories would also become (optionally?) exported information.

In terms of GUI implementation, I imagine something like a searchbox that can filter by category, and/or one tab per category (but this could become unwieldy with too many categories). Anyway, the details are not important right now.

The main thing is starting some discussion about adding the ability for the user to categorize entries in their address book, which makes looking something we know the category of but can't remember the precise entry feasible for larger addressbooks.
