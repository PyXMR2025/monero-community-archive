## Dvd-Znf | 2023-09-02T08:33:32+00:00
As quoted from the README:   
“XMRig is a high performance, open source, cross platform RandomX, KawPow, CryptoNight and [GhostRider](https://github.com/xmrig/xmrig/tree/master/src/crypto/ghostrider#readme) unified CPU/GPU miner and [RandomX benchmark](https://xmrig.com/benchmark).”   
Bitcoin uses neither of those algorithms.   
You can try to mine monero instead, which is what mainly this miner is most used for. 
It’s in the name XMRig 
Also as a side-note, you’ll never get a chance to mine Btc on a dual core cpu, even Monero would probably be less profit than what your electricity would cost.  
So I would stay away from any mining software claiming to be cabable of profitabily mining Btc from your CPU.

If this clarified your question please close this issue.
