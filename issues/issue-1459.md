# Original Description
I'm proposing a few site tweaks to increase social engagement. I think it's important that social media engagement options be more prominent on the site because social engagement (i.e. users following the Monero twitter, Facebook, joining a Monero chat) is the engagement method that the vast, vast majority (90%+?) of users will do over anything else (and normally to the exclusion of anything else). We should make it easy for them, as this majority of users is what powers social media presence and social media outreach.

Here's what I propose:

1. **Home page** - Add "Join our hangouts" or similar text as the first subsection under the "Join the Community" header, with appropriate text and a link to the https://www.getmonero.org/community/hangouts/ page.

2. **Home page** - Also consider putting a line of icons for social channels on the bottom of the footer of the Monero main page.  Having the logos on the bottom of the page is an established website pattern that allows people to quickly find them without having to even read the page (yes, I am saying that :p).

3. **On "Hangouts" page** - I am aware that we are getting a Monero Discord set up. Once this is done, I propose we change "IRC Channels" to "Chat Channels" (as well as make the relevant body text changes). Consider linking to Discord for the channel names instead of IRC (and have IRC as one of the logos to the right of the channel name). Or, minimally, add the Discord icon to that section, as well as to the list of icons at the top. My reasoning is that Discord seems to currently be the (or one of the) most well-established chat options in the crypto community. As all of these venues are relayed, we should feature the most well-known venue (that the majority of users already have the software for) first.

4. **On "Hangouts" page** - Put the Chat Channels section before the Workgroup Resources and Mailing Lists sections.

5. **On "Hangouts" page** - Add links to Telegram chat, Twitch and Discord chat on the top line of icons, once available. Telegram could be totally disconnected from the IRC channels to avoid spam/idiots (but should be moderated). Twitch and Discord can probably be bridged?
