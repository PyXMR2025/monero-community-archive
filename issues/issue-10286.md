## hhartzer | 2026-02-05T04:18:44+00:00
I've been messing with this, and Monero on OpenBSD is a mess.

Even stopping the daemon will lock up the system for minutes sometimes. You have to use certain sync options as well.

It can function, but it's tricky, and the settings to make it workable make it touchy. If the power is cut, lmdb will be corrupt and you have to start over.

## hhartzer | 2026-02-05T23:00:10+00:00
@justanotherscript There's some mailing list discussion (ports at openbsd.org) that may be helpful, but I'll share my settings.

This is unfortunately pretty mandatory: `--db-sync-mode=fast:async:10000`

If you crash with this, or lose power, you'll likely have to resync the database.

I have tinkered with that setting and never had any other workable setting that is safer.

Also, if you have too many nodes, it seems to die. I think OpenBSD 7.8 crashes or hangs, but I had to limit the number of nodes either way. `--in-peers=10 --out-peers=10` -- 20, or 40 may be fine. But don't expect a whole lot.

Stopping the daemon, properly, has taken up to 45 minutes for me. You may be able to do a `kill -HUP`, wait 30 seconds, then `kill -KILL` after that. But the system may not be responsive enough to second that second signal. I haven't tested this -- it may also kill the database.

It really does make the whole system unresponsive. Don't plan on running anything remotely important on the same server. That said, it does work under vmd and doesn't hang the host.
