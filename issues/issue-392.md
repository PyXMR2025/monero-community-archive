# Original Description
### "Policy & Regulation" Think Tank / Working Group
Over the last years and especially last months, several people in independent circles and channels around the Monero community have expressed the desire to set up a "Policy & Regulation" entity (wording mine, pardon me if I misrepresent all ideas around).

A [reddit thread](https://www.reddit.com/r/Monero/comments/d5igia/monero_regulatory_workgroup) was posted on the topic. Please read it, there are some interesting comments there.



**Practical examples of initiatives in scope**
To start discussion towards thinking what this entity could aim at, here are some items that I spew from the top of my head. These are both past/current/possible future initiatives that IMO could potentially be within scope.

* the #eu-crypto-privacy discussions that was set up about a year ago (I think?) when some high-level EU technocrats made noise about banning some crypto primitives such as ring signatures.

* the initiative of Midipoet to follow and take part in discussions of two working groups of the ITU (UN telecommunications subgroup), ["Digital Currency"](https://extranet.itu.int/sites/itu-t/focusgroups/dfc/SitePages/Home.aspx) & ["Application of Distributed Ledger Technology"](https://extranet.itu.int/sites/itu-t/focusgroups/fgdlt/SitePages/Home.aspx).

* I am personally interested to design (on paper only, I do not mean coding) a "GDPR-coin", that respects both the GDPR and the principle of decentralization, if at all possible. Then analyze how Monero fits with respect to these theoretical specifications.
Or just show directly that Monero is the most GPDR-compliant cryptocurrency, but it's less fun of an approach :)

* Get in touch with EU institutions. We have no recurring channel of communications or contacts, this is stupid. Last year I got to a panel within parliament on cashless societies, this was a useful event. But the organizers struggled to get a hold of someone in the community willing to go and it worked with some luck this time; how many missed opportunities of that type? 
I mention EU as I am mostly familiar with the institutions in Brussels, but the same is true for any other relevant policy center.

* Analyse existing reports and write complementary (contradictory?) papers. For instance a large report ["Blockchain Now and Tomorrow"](https://ec.europa.eu/jrc/en/publication/eur-scientific-and-technical-research-reports/blockchain-now-and-tomorrow) from the Joint Research Center of the European Commission was just released. I skimmed through the executive summary only and it already warrants some reactions.

* KYC (there is a growing debate that applying plain simple passport-KYC is sometimes quite detrimental -- see all the data breach and the ID documents linked).

* Blockchain analytics companies: they have a vested interest to paint the crypto world in a way that makes analysing blockchain history on top of normal KYC necessary and mandatory. This is antagonist to the idea of fungibility and Monero users should fight back this narrative.

* The new FATF recommendations, and in particular the travel rule. So many open questions on this one.


**Key meta questions** 
xmrhaelan posted this list of key initial questions:

* Do we need a separate workgroup for this or should it fall within the current Monero Outreach Workgroup?

* What directions, ideas, priorities exist among Monero that relate to policy and regulation?

* How should we organize going forward? Do we need to setup another IRC channel for communication or can we use the existing Monero Outreach channels and stick with periodic community updates?

* What other considerations should be made?


There were several poeple answering in the reddit thread. Maybe somebody can go through before the meeting and summarize the positions, and then further points and opinions be raised at the meeting?

AJS added (in private) the following important question:

* To which extent should it be Monero specific vs. cryptocurrency agnostic?

**Now let's get to an effective agenda**

So I think maybe if everyone could chime in on (i) whatever you have in mind that this entity could be interested about or try to do in practice, so we discuss in terms that are not too abstract, and (ii) general questions and items that definitely should be covered during meeting, please add to the list.
Hopefully we will get to a constructive agenda, to be finalized in time for meeting.

_Note that we will not necessarily keep having meetings in the future. Everything is up to be decided. This one is more of a meta meeting for defining everything else going forward._

On a personal note I'm super excited about things moving on this front, and I know many who are as well. Let's put FUNgibility back into policies and regulations!
