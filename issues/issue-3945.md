> Did you find all 25 words in this list? https://github.com/monero-project/monero/blob/master/src/mnemonics/english.h

Seems like a manual task that would be vulnerable to human error.
Why not just get the computer to do that for you? 
Right there in the editor/cmdline. Even get it to show you the words that are missing?

For those that don't program in C or have severe dyslexia use the following command 

> tr -s '[:space:]' '\n' < mykey.txt | sort | join -v1 -j1 - bip39.txt

[bip39.txt](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/24146497/bip39.txt)

with a little effort you can use aspell

> aspell --personal=./bip39.dict check mykey.txt

I also believe that it's only the first 4 chars that represent the word (something to do with UTF-8, bytes and big integer data types I presume)
